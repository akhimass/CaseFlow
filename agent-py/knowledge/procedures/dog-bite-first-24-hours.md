First 24 hours after a dog bite or animal attack. Get medical attention right away — bite wounds carry serious infection and rabies risk, and prompt treatment also documents the injury. Report the bite to animal control or local authorities; this creates an official record and triggers a check on the dog's vaccination and bite history. Identify the dog's owner and get their name, address, and contact information, plus any proof of the dog's rabies vaccination if available. Photograph your injuries and the location where it happened, and note any witnesses. Save torn or bloodied clothing. Keep all medical records and bills, and speak with a personal-injury attorney before discussing the incident with the owner's insurer.
