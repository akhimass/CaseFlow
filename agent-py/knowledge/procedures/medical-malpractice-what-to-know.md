Considering a medical-malpractice claim — what to know before contacting a lawyer. Be honest with yourself about the bar: a bad outcome is not the same as malpractice. You must show the provider fell below the accepted professional standard of care and that this caused your injury — which nearly always requires another qualified physician to review the records and testify. These cases are expensive and slow to build, so most attorneys screen them carefully and take only strong ones. Act early: deadlines are shorter than ordinary injury cases and several states require advance notice to the provider before filing, and some cap non-economic damages. Start gathering your complete medical records now, write down the timeline of what happened while it is fresh, and avoid signing any settlement or release from the provider or its insurer before an attorney reviews it.
