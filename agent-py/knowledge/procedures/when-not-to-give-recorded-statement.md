When not to give a recorded statement. You are generally not obligated to provide a recorded statement to the at-fault party's insurance company. Recorded statements are frequently used to lock in inconsistencies or premature admissions before injuries are fully diagnosed. Decline politely, say you will follow up in writing or through counsel, and consult an attorney first. Your own insurer's cooperation clause is narrower than adjusters often imply.
