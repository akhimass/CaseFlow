California medical-malpractice claim, San Diego. A retained surgical instrument after an abdominal operation caused infection, a second corrective surgery, and lasting complications. High severity with clear deviation from the standard of care confirmed by an expert reviewer. Note California's MICRA cap limits non-economic ("pain and suffering") damages — $390,000 for non-death cases as of 2024, rising annually — so most of the value sits in economic damages: corrective care, future medical costs, and lost earnings. Total settlement range $500,000–$2,000,000 driven by economic loss, not pain-and-suffering.
