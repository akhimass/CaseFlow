California workplace injury with a third-party personal-injury claim, Oakland. A delivery driver employed by another company struck a warehouse worker on a loading dock. Because the at-fault party was not the worker's employer, this is a personal-injury claim against the third party (in addition to any workers'-comp benefits), so the usual PI damages — including pain and suffering — are recoverable. Medium severity: shoulder tear requiring arthroscopic surgery and several months of restricted duty. Liability on the third-party driver was clear. Settlement range $80,000–$250,000, coordinated with the workers'-comp lien.
