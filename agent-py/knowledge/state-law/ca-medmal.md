California medical-malpractice primer. Med-mal is among the hardest PI cases to prove: the plaintiff must show the provider deviated from the professional standard of care and that the deviation caused the injury, almost always requiring qualified expert testimony. Set honest expectations. Three rules dominate. First, the statute of limitations (Code Civ. Proc. §340.5): one year from when the patient discovered (or should have discovered) the injury, and no more than three years from the injury itself, whichever is first — shorter than ordinary PI. Second, a plaintiff must serve the provider with 90 days' notice of intent to sue before filing (§364). Third, MICRA caps non-economic damages (Civ. Code §3333.2): $390,000 for non-death cases in 2024, increasing annually toward $750,000 — economic damages (medical costs, lost earnings) are not capped, so case value concentrates there.
