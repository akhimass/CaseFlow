California follows pure comparative negligence. An injured plaintiff may recover damages even if they are mostly at fault; the award is reduced by their own percentage of fault (Li v. Yellow Cab Co., 1975). A plaintiff found 30% at fault in a rear-end collision still recovers 70% of damages. There is no fault-percentage bar to recovery, which makes contested-fault auto cases still viable in California.
