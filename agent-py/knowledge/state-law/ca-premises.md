California premises-liability primer. Property owners and occupiers owe a general duty of reasonable care to keep premises safe (Civ. Code §1714; Rowland v. Christian, 1968, abolished the old rigid visitor categories). To recover for a slip-, trip-, or fall, the injured person generally must show the owner created the dangerous condition or had actual or constructive notice of it and failed to fix or warn within a reasonable time. California's pure comparative negligence applies, so a plaintiff partly at fault (e.g. not watching where they stepped) still recovers, reduced by their share. The standard filing window is two years (Code Civ. Proc. §335.1). Critical exception: claims against a public entity (a city, county, or state property) require a written government claim filed within six months of the injury (Gov. Code §911.2) before any lawsuit — missing it can bar the case.
