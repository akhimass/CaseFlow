Florida does not cap economic or non-economic damages in ordinary personal injury cases. Statutory caps on non-economic damages in medical malpractice were held unconstitutional by the Florida Supreme Court (N. Broward Hosp. Dist. v. Kalitan, 2017). Florida is a no-fault auto state: Personal Injury Protection (PIP) provides $10,000 in benefits, and a plaintiff must meet a serious-injury threshold to sue for non-economic damages (Fla. Stat. §627.737).
