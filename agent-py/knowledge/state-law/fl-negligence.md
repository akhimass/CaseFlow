---

As of HB 837 (effective March 24, 2023), Florida switched from pure comparative negligence to modified comparative negligence with a 51% bar (Fla. Stat. §768.81). A plaintiff more than 50% at fault for their own injuries generally recovers nothing; at 50% or less, damages are reduced by the plaintiff's fault percentage. Medical malpractice cases are an exception and remain pure comparative.
