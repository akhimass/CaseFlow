Texas uses modified comparative negligence, called proportionate responsibility, with a 51% bar (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §33.001). A plaintiff who is 50% or less at fault recovers damages reduced by their fault percentage; a plaintiff found 51% or more at fault recovers nothing. This makes establishing the other party's majority fault critical in contested auto cases.
