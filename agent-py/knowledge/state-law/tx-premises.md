Texas premises-liability primer. Unlike California, Texas still classifies entrants and the duty owed depends on the category. An invitee (a customer in a store) is owed the highest duty: the owner must use reasonable care to protect against dangerous conditions it knew or reasonably should have known about. A licensee (a social guest) is owed a lesser duty — to warn of or fix conditions the owner actually knows about. A trespasser is owed only the duty not to injure willfully or through gross negligence. An invitee plaintiff generally must prove the owner had actual or constructive knowledge of the hazard, the condition posed an unreasonable risk, and the owner failed to exercise reasonable care. Texas applies modified comparative fault (barred if more than 50% at fault), and the filing window is two years (§16.003).
